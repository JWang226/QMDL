    Core Concepts¶

    Free Probability & Free Entropy¶

    • Introduction to Free Probability, Free Entropy, and Free Entropy Dimension — self-contained pedagogical introduction
    • Free Probability Theory — the broader mathematical framework
    • Semicircular Element and Free Independence — non-commutative Gaussian and freeness
    • Free Entropy — Voiculescu's non-commutative Shannon entropy
    • Physical Free Entropy — finite-dimensional, resolution-dependent version
    • Free Entropy Dimension — leading-order scaling coefficient
    • Regularized Free Entropy — the \(O(1)\) eigenvalue-dependent correction
    • Vandermonde Determinant — eigenvalue repulsion factor

    Information Theory & Compression¶

    • Quantum Minimum Description Length — the compression task and main result
    • Kolmogorov Complexity & QKC — classical and quantum descriptive complexity
    • Schumacher Compression — contrasting: von Neumann entropy governs this
    • Covering Numbers — Kolmogorov \(\varepsilon\)-entropy underlying physical free entropy

    Representation Theory¶

    • Schur-Weyl Duality & Schur Transform — the fundamental decomposition and its unitary implementation
    • Young Diagrams and Partitions — labels for irreps
    • Schur Polynomials — characters of GL(d) representations
    • Gelfand-Tsetlin Basis — canonical basis for GL(d) irreps
    • Weyl Dimension Formula — dimension of irreps
    • PRV Component — Parthasarathy-Ranga Rao-Varadarajan component
    • Kumar's Theorem — multiplicity-one for PRV components
    • Casimir Operator — used in perturbation analysis
    • Kostant Partition Function — bounds weight multiplicities
    • Flag Manifold — the eigenbasis manifold

    Cloning & Compression¶

    • Generalized Cloning Map — the key technical innovation
    • Werner's Cloning Map — the qubit special case
    • YCH Scheme — Yang-Chiribella-Hayashi precursor
    • Koashi-Imoto Structure Theorem — for the converse proof
    • Petz Recovery Map — reverse cloner interpretation
    • Holevo Information — used in converse proofs

    Geometric Quantization¶

    • KKS Theorem — explains the factor of 1/2 between free entropy and QMDL
